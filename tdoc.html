TDoc

TDoc was born out of a bootcamp project, but it was first conceived from personal, lived experiences of the gap in healthcare access for transgender and gender non-conforming patients across the United States.

I wanted to create an app specifically designed for the TGNC community (as well as caregivers of TGNC children) that would help ease the burden of finding and vetting safe, supportive healthcare providers.

The Problem

Because of the stigma, assumptions, and discrimination faced when navigating the U.S. healthcare system, many TGNC (trans and gender non-conforming) patients find themselves educating doctors on their needs, being incorrectly named/gendered in medical settings, and asked invasive and irrelevant questions related to their bodies. These repeated negative experiences (microaggressions) make TGNC patients less likely than cisgender (non-trans) patients to seek out healthcare, and has an overall negative impact on physical and mental well-being.

The Solution

TGNC individuals need a streamlined way to locate and endorse competent and affirming healthcare providers, even when they aren't receiving explicitly gender-affirming services. I wanted to create an app specifically designed for the TGNC community (as well as caregivers of TGNC children) that would help ease the burden of finding and vetting safe, supportive healthcare providers.

Enter: the TDoc app!
